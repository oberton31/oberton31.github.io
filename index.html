Penalty Kicking Humanoid

Pittsburgh, PA | January 2025 - April 2025

Project Overview

Our objective was to simulate a penalty-style soccer kick with a Unitree G1 Humanoid Robot and kick the ball at a specified target. To accomplish this, in Julia, we designed and implemented an optimal control framework for generating and stabilizing dynamic kicking motions on the Unitree G1 humanoid robot using direct collocation trajectory optimization. This project was done as part of the Optimal Control and Reinforcement Learning Course at Carnegie Mellon University.

Contributions

  • Implemented direct collocation trajectory optimization to generate the optimal kicking trajectory.
  • Implemented a QP solver to determine the optimal foot location and impulse to use when striking the ball.
  • Designed a Time-Varying LQR controller to track the generated trajectory, and an Infinite-Horizon LQR controller to stabilize the robot before and after the kick.
  • Achieved 90% accuracy on simulated test kicks.
Julia Optimal Control LQR Trajectory Optimization Optimization Quadratic Programming
